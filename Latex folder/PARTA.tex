{\Large \textbf{ABSTRACT}}\\[1cm]
\end{center}
\onehalfspacing
\noindent
The Industry Fitness DeepTech Internship Program 2026, conducted by SMPS Electric Control Pvt. Ltd.under the SMPS Tech Lab division, was designed to bridge the gap between academic learning and real-world industrial application. The program aimed to develop industry-ready engineers through technical training, practical exposure, and innovation-driven activities. It followed a structured model combining Research \& Development (R\&D) and Entrepreneurship, helping interns understand both engineering solutions and product development.\\[5mm]
\noindent
During the internship, training sessions were conducted on electronics fundamentals, digital electronics, microcontrollers, embedded systems, RISC and CISC architecture, ARM basics, and industrial engineering practices. These sessions strengthened core concepts and improved practical readiness. As part of the final evaluation, special focus was given to 8051 Microcontroller \& Embedded Systems, covering theory, programming, hardware interfacing, simulation, and implementation.  \\[5mm]
\noindent
The internship also included an entrepreneurship activity where a prototype platform titled Scan2Crack – ECE Edition was conceptualized for engineering interview preparation. This provided exposure to idea validation, product planning, user-focused design, and software development using Python and Streamlit. \\[5mm] 
\noindent
In the R\&D phase, I worked in a team on the project titled Forward Engineering \& Development of AC-DC SMPS Power Supply. The work involved studying SMPS architecture, analyzing a reference system, understanding stages such as rectification, switching, transformer isolation, output filtering, and feedback regulation. Additional activities included schematic recreation, footprint assignment, component analysis, and basic learning of PCB design workflow using professional tools. \\[5mm] 
\noindent
Overall, the internship was a valuable platform for improving technical knowledge, practical skills, teamwork, communication, and professional confidence. It prepared me for future opportunities in embedded systems, electronics design, research, and core engineering industries.
